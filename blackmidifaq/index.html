Black MIDI FAQ

In this page you will find several questions and answers about many Black MIDI topics!

1

Q: What is Black MIDI?

A: Black MIDI is a music genre which uses the MIDI protocol. Usually they consist of thousands or millions of notes containing music, accompaniments or note art. The term “black” comes from the fact that the dense notes in the MIDI apear as black on sheet music.

2

Q: How can I make a Black MIDI?

In order to make a Black MIDI, “blackers” (term for the people who create Black MIDIs) create a song or remix an existing one (they either have to transcribe it or use an existing base) and then in a MIDI sequencer of they choice, they add several layers of melody accompaniments that all blend together (something like an orchestra). Additionally, some blackers add note art on top of the sound (note art consists of many notes connected to create different shapes to make the MIDI look interesting).

3

Q: What software can I use to create Black MIDIs?

Some of the most used and well known programs that blackers choose to use to create Black MIDIs are:

  1. Domino (free): http://takabosoft.com/domino *
  2. FLStudio ($99): https://www.image-line.com/
  3. Sekaiju (free): https://openmidiproject.osdn.jp/index_en.html
*Domino is in Japanese but there is an English translation which can be downloaded here: https://github.com/Hans5958/Domino-English-Translation/releases/

4

Q: How can I make videos of Black MIDIs?

There is a number of programs required in order to record or render Black MIDI videos.

MIDI players (used to play back MIDIs):

  1. Piano From Above: https://kaleidonkep99.altervista.org/pfamirror/index.html
  2. Kiva: https://github.com/arduano/Kiva/releases/
  3. Synthesia: https://synthesiagame.com/
  4. MIDITrail: https://osdn.net/projects/miditrail/

MIDI Drivers (used to create audio for the MIDIs that the MIDI players play):

  1. OmniMIDI (recommended): https://github.com/KeppySoftware/OmniMIDI/releases
  2. VirtualMIDISynth: https://coolsoft.altervista.org/en/virtualmidisynth

MIDI Renderers (used to generate no-lag videos of MIDIs):

  1. Zenith: https://github.com/arduano/Zenith-MIDI/releases
  2. Ultralight MIDI Player: https://pipiraworld.web.fc2.com/ump/en.html
  3. TGM’s Video Generator: https://github.com/89Mods/TheGhastModdings-MIDI-Video-Generator/releases

MIDI Audio Renderers (used to generate no-lag perfect audio recordings of MIDIs):

  1. Keppy’s MIDI Converter: https://github.com/KeppySoftware/KMC/releases
  2. Synthfont 2: http://www.synthfont.com/

 

After recording the MIDI player or render a video, you need to use an audio renderer to generate sound and then use a video editor of your choice to sync the video with the audio.

5

Q: Where can I download MIDIs and Soundfonts?

A good place to get files from is the Black MIDI Community Discord server. There are channels for both MIDI (#midi-files) and soundfont (#soundfonts) sharing. Aside from this you can find my Black MIDIs and soundfont projects in this website.
