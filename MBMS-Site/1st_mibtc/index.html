1st MBMSP International Blacking Technique Competition

Information

The 1st MBMSP International Blacking Technique Competition (MIBTC) is a competition in which blackers from all over the world can show their blacking skills to the whole Black MIDI Community. Everyone will black the same song within the period of 2 months (1st April 2020 – 30th June 2020) and when they are done, they will submit their MIDI to us through a form. Then the 6 judges listed below will evaluate each MIDI and score it. After about 1 or 2 weeks after the submissions are closed (30 June) the results will be announced both in the Black MIDI Community Discord server and in a showcase video, which will show all the MIDIs by all creators, which will be uploaded in the MBMS channel.

Judges

Song

The task song (as described above) will be “OOO” from the game Beatmania IIDX 24 written by Ryu (https://youtu.be/1CBQm9jcr5E). Each blacker will be given a base MIDI file (in piano tutorial form) and another MIDI file which contains the correct chords from the song. The MIDI arrangement we will provide is created by ShinkoNet and modified by MBMS. You do not have to use the files we provide. You can make your own arrangement or find the chords on your own. Although, you HAVE to create a Black MIDI of the song OOO by Ryu. 

Rules

In order to get your submission accepted, you have to follow certain rules:

  1. You must black with your own personal style. There is no standard that you have to follow. The MIDI must be created entirely by you using your own style and techniques of blacking.
  2. The note count must be higher than 50k and smaller than 10 million. I think the reason that there is a limit of the notecount is obvious. Even though the showcase video will contain no-lag edited recordings of every MIDI in Piano From Above, we don’t want the judge’s computer to suffer, right?
  3. You must NOT share any previews of your work while the submissions are open. That means that from 1st April 2020 to 30th June 2020 you are not allowed to share previews of your work in MIDI, video, audio, image or any form with others. I mean, you want to win don’t you?
  4. You may use any General MIDI instrument of your choice. Even though this is a Black MIDI (normally we use only piano), you are allowed to use whatever instrument you think fits the song best. Although, keep in mind that the main instrument has to be the piano.
  5. You must black only the song that is set. Any other songs will be disqualified. As stated above, Black MIDIs of other songs will be disqualified from the contest.
  6. Any entries after 30 June 2020 at 00:00 (UTC+02:00) will also be disqualified.

 

If you don’t follow one or more of these rules, your submission will be disqualified.

Scoring

The scoring system is divided in two categories. One for Korean-style MIDIs and one for Visual-based note art MIDIs. Each MIDI style has 5 scoring criteria. Each of the 5 parts will be scored on a scale from 1 to 10, with 300 the best score for a MIDI ([5parts*10pts]*6judges).

Korean-style MIDIs

  1. Melody
  2. Audio
  3. Visuals
  4. Transitions
  5. Variety/Creativity

Visual-based MIDIs

  1. Audio
  2. Visuals
  3. Transitions
  4. Fittingness
  5. Balance
